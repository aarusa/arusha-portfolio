My Journey to Salesforce Marketing Cloud

  • By Arusha Shahi

When I first heard about Marketing Cloud, I honestly thought it was just another digital marketing tool. Since I come from a software engineering background, I didn't pay much attention to it. Marketing felt like a completely different field, and I didn't see how it fit into my career. But after facing multiple failed attempts to land a job in software engineering, I decided to give Marketing Cloud a chance.

I started with the basics—learning simple terms and understanding what the platform actually does. At that point, I was still not fully convinced. Everything changed when I finally got access to the system. With the help of my mentor, I was able to get a subscription and start exploring it hands-on.

Under my mentor’s guidance (who is a professional cloud developer in this field), I began to understand things much faster. That’s when I realized that Salesforce Marketing Cloud is not just about marketing—it’s a powerful platform with many technical aspects and different career paths.

I seriously started learning Salesforce Marketing Cloud in February 2026. It’s only been about a month, but since getting access, I’ve been learning and practicing every day. Recently, I also started learning through Trailhead, which has helped me strengthen my fundamentals.

So far, I’ve covered the basics of Email Studio, Mobile Studio, CloudPages, Content Builder, Contact Builder, Automations, Journeys, and even started building custom SFMC tools. This has given me the confidence that I can now step into the job market and apply for entry-level roles. My next goal is to take the Email Specialist certification next month, and then move on to becoming a Marketing Cloud Developer.

Looking back, I never thought I would shift towards something like this. But with the right guidance and consistent effort, things started to make sense. This is just the beginning of my journey, and I’m excited to see where it takes me.
